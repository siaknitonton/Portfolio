Getting Started with Arduino: Programming and Virtual Circuits in Tinkercad

Training Fee: ₱ 300
Duration: 5 hours (2 day) - 2.5 Hours per day

View Course Details

The "Getting Started with Arduino: Programming and Virtual Circuits in Tinkercad" is designed to introduce learners to the fundamentals of Arduino programming and circuit simulation using Tinkercad. This course is ideal for beginners with basic knowledge of electronic circuit connections who are eager to explore microcontroller programming.

Through step-by-step tutorials and hands-on projects, participants will learn how to:

  • Understand the basics of Arduino hardware and its components
  • Write and upload simple Arduino programs
  • Simulate electronic circuits using Tinkercad's virtual environment
  • Develop practical projects such as LED control, motor interfacing, and sensor integration

The course also covers essential concepts like digital and analog signals, basic programming structures, and serial communication. Learners will gain insights into how sensors and actuators interact with Arduino boards, enhancing their understanding of embedded systems.

In addition to technical skills, this course encourages problem-solving and project-based learning. By building and testing circuits in Tinkercad, participants can experiment safely without physical hardware, making it an ideal platform for both beginners and educators.

By the end of this course, learners will have the confidence to build simple Arduino-based projects and will be equipped with the foundational knowledge required to advance in electronics, robotics, and automation.

What you’ll learn

  • Understand Arduino Basics: Identify and explain key Arduino components.
  • Design Circuits: Create and simulate circuits using Tinkercad.
  • Write Code: Develop and debug Arduino code for hardware control.
  • Build Projects: Implement simple Arduino-based projects.

Are there any course requirements or prerequisites?

  • Basic knowledge of electronic circuit connections.

Who this course is for:

  • Beginners interested in learning Arduino and Tinkercad for electronics projects.
  • Students and hobbyists looking to explore basic circuit design and programming.
  • Anyone with basic electronic circuit knowledge who wants to build practical Arduino projects through simulation.
